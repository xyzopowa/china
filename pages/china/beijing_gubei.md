## Gubei Water Town (古北水镇)

Gubei Water Town is a beautifully restored traditional Chinese water town located at the base of the Simatai section of the Great Wall. It combines ancient architecture with modern tourist facilities, offering a unique overnight experience near the Great Wall.

## Overview

- **Location**: Miyun District, 2 hours from central Beijing
- **Admission**: ¥140 (includes town access, not Great Wall)
- **Best For**: Overnight stay, photography, Great Wall visit
- **Duration**: Full day or overnight recommended

## What to See and Do

### Water Town Attractions

- **Traditional Architecture**: Ming and Qing dynasty style buildings
- **Canals and Bridges**: Picturesque waterways throughout the town
- **Ancient Dyeing Workshop**: See traditional cloth dyeing techniques
- **Yongshun Dyehouse**: Historic textile production site
- **Night Lighting**: Beautiful illumination after dark

### Simatai Great Wall

The town provides access to the dramatic Simatai section of the Great Wall, known for its steep inclines and original features.

- **Night Visit**: Unique opportunity to visit illuminated Great Wall
- **Cable Car**: Available to reach the wall
- **Hiking**: Challenging but rewarding terrain

## Accommodation

Stay overnight in one of the town's boutique hotels or guesthouses to experience the magical evening atmosphere and catch sunrise at the Great Wall.

**Options**:
- Wtown Riverside Hotel
- Traditional courtyard guesthouses
- Budget inns

## Activities

- **Hot Springs**: Relax in natural hot spring pools
- **Cultural Shows**: Evening performances
- **Local Cuisine**: Try regional specialties
- **Photography**: Excellent photo opportunities, especially at dusk

## Getting There

### From Beijing

**Private Car/Taxi**:
- Duration: 2-2.5 hours
- Most convenient option
- Can be arranged through hotels

**Public Transportation**:
- Bus from Dongzhimen Station
- More budget-friendly but less convenient

**Tour Packages**:
- Many travel agencies offer day trips
- Often combined with Great Wall visit

## Tips

- **Stay Overnight**: To fully experience the town and avoid day trip crowds
- **Visit in Evening**: The lighting is spectacular after dark
- **Book Advance**: Hotels fill up quickly on weekends
- **Comfortable Shoes**: Cobblestone streets throughout
- **Weather**: Can be cold in winter, hot in summer

## Dining

- **Local Restaurants**: Serve regional Beijing cuisine
- **Street Food**: Try various snacks along the canals
- **Hotel Dining**: Quality options in larger hotels

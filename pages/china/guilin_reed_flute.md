## Reed Flute Cave

A spectacular limestone cave filled with colorful lighting and fascinating rock formations.

### About the Cave

- **Length**: 240 meters deep
- **Age**: Over 180 million years old
- **Name**: From reeds growing outside used to make flutes
- **Formations**: Stalactites, stalagmites, stone pillars

### The Experience

**Colorful Illumination**
- Multicolored lights enhance formations
- Creates otherworldly atmosphere
- Photography allowed (no flash recommended)
- Paths well-maintained and safe

**Notable Formations**
- Crystal Palace
- Virgin Forest
- Dragon Pagoda
- Various named rock formations requiring imagination

**Inscriptions**
- Ancient ink writings on cave walls
- Dating back to Tang Dynasty (792 AD)
- Shows cave visited for over 1200 years

### Visiting Information

- **Duration**: 1 hour guided tour
- **Access**: About 5 km from Guilin city center
- **How to Get There**: Taxi, bus, or tour
- **Best Time**: Morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds

### Tips

- Tours are guided only (Chinese and sometimes English)
- Can be crowded - go early or late
- Slippery in places - wear good shoes
- Temperature inside cave cooler than outside
- Photography works well with cave lighting
- Part of many Guilin city tours

### Combining Visits

Often combined with:
- Seven Star Park
- Elephant Trunk Hill
- City tours

### Worth It?

Yes, if you enjoy caves - it's one of China's most famous and well-presented cave systems.

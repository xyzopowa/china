## About Guangzhou

Guangzhou (Canton) is the capital of Guangdong Province and one of China's most important southern cities. As the birthplace of Cantonese culture and cuisine, Guangzhou offers a different flavor of China - more relaxed, food-obsessed, and with a subtropical climate that sets it apart from northern cities.

## Why Visit

- **Food Paradise**: Home of authentic Cantonese cuisine and world-class dim sum
- **Trading History**: Ancient Silk Road port, historic trading quarters
- **Colonial Architecture**: Shamian Island's European buildings
- **Modern Skyline**: Canton Tower and contemporary architecture
- **Pearl River**: Evening cruises and waterfront promenades
- **Accessible**: Easy day trip or stopover between Hong Kong and mainland
- **Authentic**: Less touristy than Beijing or Shanghai

## Key Highlights

### Cultural & Historical

- **Chen Clan Ancestral Hall**: Exquisite traditional architecture
- **Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall**: Tribute to the founder of modern China
- **Shamian Island**: European colonial architecture
- **Beijing Road**: Ancient road with visible layers of history
- **Sacred Heart Cathedral**: Largest Gothic church in China

### Modern & Scenic

- **Canton Tower**: Iconic twisted tower with observation deck
- **Pearl River Night Cruise**: See illuminated skyline
- **Huacheng Square**: Modern civic center
- **Redtory Art District**: Creative industrial renovation

### Food & Markets

- **Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street**: Shopping and street food
- **Qingping Market**: Traditional market (controversial wildlife section)
- **Tea Houses**: Traditional Cantonese morning tea culture
- **Countless Restaurants**: Best Cantonese food in the world

## Best Time to Visit

- **Autumn (October-November)**: Pleasant temperatures, less humid
- **Winter (December-February)**: Mild, comfortable for walking
- **Spring (March-May)**: Warm, increasingly humid, beautiful flowers
- **Summer (June-September)**: Hot, humid, rainy (typhoon season)

**Best Months**: October-November or March-April

## How Many Days?

- **Quick Visit**: 1-2 days (highlights + food)
- **Recommended**: 2-3 days (main sights + food exploration)
- **Comprehensive**: 3-4 days (includes day trips)

## Getting There

### By Air
- **Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN)**: Major international hub
- Direct flights from worldwide destinations
- Metro to city center: 40-60 minutes
- Airport Express: 30-40 minutes

### By Train
- **Guangzhou South Railway Station**: High-speed trains
  - From Hong Kong: 48 minutes
  - From Shenzhen: 30-40 minutes
  - From Shanghai: 7-8 hours
  - From Beijing: 8-9 hours
- **Guangzhou Railway Station**: Traditional trains, city center

### From Hong Kong
- High-speed train: 48 minutes (very convenient)
- Bus: 3-4 hours
- Ferry: To Nansha (less common)

## Getting Around

- **Metro**: Extensive, modern, efficient, English signs
- **Taxi/Didi**: Affordable and convenient
- **Bus**: Comprehensive but challenging for non-Chinese speakers
- **Bike Share**: Available, good for certain areas
- **Walking**: Doable in certain districts

## Language

- **Cantonese**: Local dialect (different from Mandarin)
- **Mandarin**: Widely understood
- **English**: Limited outside tourist areas and hotels
- **Note**: Even Chinese speakers may struggle if only know Mandarin

## Weather Considerations

- **Subtropical**: Hot and humid much of year
- **Rain**: Common, especially summer
- **Typhoons**: Possible June-September
- **Winter**: Mild but can be damp
- **Pack**: Light clothing, rain gear, sunscreen

## Tips

- Try morning tea (yum cha) - essential Guangzhou experience
- Don't miss evening Pearl River views
- Shamian Island perfect for leisurely stroll
- Metro is the best way to get around
- Food is the main attraction - come hungry
- Many restaurants close between lunch and dinner
- ATMs widely available
- Mobile payment dominant (but cards work at major places)

## Visa Note

- Chinese visa required (or visa-free transit if eligible)
- Easy day trip from Hong Kong if you have multi-entry visa
- Check current visa-free transit policies

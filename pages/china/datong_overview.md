## About Datong

Historic city in Shanxi Province, known for two of China's most remarkable Buddhist sites. Often visited as a stop between Beijing and Xi'an or Pingyao.

### Main Attractions

- **Yungang Grottoes**: UNESCO World Heritage Buddhist caves
- **Hanging Monastery**: Temple built into cliff face
- **Ancient City Wall**: Reconstructed Ming Dynasty walls
- **Nine Dragon Screen**: Oldest and largest glazed screen in China

### Getting There

**From Beijing**
- High-speed train: 1.5-2 hours
- Makes excellent day trip or overnight

**From Xi'an/Pingyao**
- Train connections available
- Can be included in northern route

### How Long to Visit

- **Day Trip from Beijing**: Possible for Yungang Grottoes
- **1 Night**: See both major sites
- **2 Nights**: Relaxed pace, include city sights

### Best Time

- **Spring/Fall**: Pleasant weather
- **Summer**: Hot but manageable
- **Winter**: Very cold but fewer tourists
- **Avoid**: Chinese holidays (very crowded)

## About Zhangjiajie

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park is one of China's most spectacular natural wonders, famous for its thousands of towering quartzite sandstone pillars that rise dramatically from the forest floor. The area gained international fame as the inspiration for the floating Hallelujah Mountains in James Cameron's Avatar movie.

## Why Visit

- **Avatar Mountains**: See the real-life inspiration for the movie's floating peaks
- **Unique Geology**: Thousands of sandstone pillars creating otherworldly landscapes
- **Glass Bridge**: Walk on the world's longest and highest glass bridge
- **Tianmen Mountain**: Cable car ride and cliff-hanging walkway
- **Natural Beauty**: UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning vistas
- **Bailong Elevator**: Outdoor elevator climbing 326m up a cliff face

## Key Attractions

### Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

The main park area with several scenic areas:

1. **Yuanjiajie (Avatar Hallelujah Mountain)**
   - The famous Avatar pillar
   - Stunning viewpoints
   - Natural bridge formations

2. **Tianzi Mountain**
   - "Peak of the Son of Heaven"
   - Sea of clouds views
   - Ancient village sites

3. **Golden Whip Stream**
   - Valley walk among pillars
   - Crystal clear stream
   - Easier terrain

4. **Yangjiajie**
   - Less crowded than other areas
   - Natural bridge
   - One-Pillar-Holding-the-South-Heaven

### Tianmen Mountain

Separate from the main park:
- **Heaven's Gate Cave**: Natural arch in the mountain
- **Glass Skywalk**: Cliff-edge walkway with glass floor
- **Tianmen Mountain Cable Car**: World's longest mountain cable car
- **99 Bends Road**: Dramatic hairpin road to the summit

### Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge

- World's longest and highest glass bridge
- 430m long, 300m above the canyon floor
- Opened in 2016
- Thrilling walking experience

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (April-May)**: Fresh greenery, comfortable weather, some rain
- **Autumn (September-November)**: Clear skies, best visibility, autumn colors
- **Summer (June-August)**: Peak season, crowded, occasional rain, lush and green
- **Winter (December-February)**: Snow and ice possible, fewer tourists, mystical atmosphere

**Best Months**: October-November for clearest weather and beautiful autumn scenery

## How Many Days?

- **Minimum**: 2 days (main park only)
- **Recommended**: 3 days (park + Tianmen Mountain)
- **Comprehensive**: 4 days (park + Tianmen + Glass Bridge + relaxed pace)

## Getting There

### By Air
- **Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (DYG)**: Flights from major Chinese cities
- 10-15 minutes from city center
- Airport buses and taxis available

### By Train
- **Zhangjiajie Railway Station**: High-speed trains from major cities
  - From Changsha: ~3 hours
  - From Guangzhou: ~6 hours
  - From Shanghai: ~11 hours

### From Other Destinations
- From Fenghuang: ~4 hours by bus
- From Changsha: ~4 hours by road

## Getting Around

- **Park Shuttle Buses**: Included with park entrance
- **Cable Cars**: Several in the park (separate tickets)
- **Bailong Elevator**: Fastest way up the cliffs
- **Walking**: Extensive trails and stairs

## Entrance Fees

- **National Forest Park**: 짜228 (valid for 4 days)
- **Tianmen Mountain**: 짜261 (includes cable car)
- **Glass Bridge**: 짜138
- **Cable cars/elevators in park**: 짜50-72 each

## Tips

- Stay overnight on the mountain to catch sunrise/sunset
- Book Bailong Elevator and cable cars in advance during peak season
- Wear comfortable hiking shoes
- Weather changes quickly - bring layers
- Start early to avoid crowds
- Download offline maps - signal can be weak
- Bring snacks and water - prices higher on mountain
- Photography equipment: bring extra batteries

## Fitness Level Required

- **Moderate**: Lots of stairs and walking
- **Cable cars/elevators**: Make it accessible for most fitness levels
- **Can avoid most stairs**: By using paid transport options
- Golden Whip Stream: Easier, flat valley walk

## Yungang Grottoes
### Grottes Yungang

UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring 51,000 Buddhist statues in 251 caves, carved during the 5th-6th centuries.

### About the Grottoes

- **Built**: 5th century AD (Northern Wei Dynasty)
- **Scale**: 251 caves, 51,000 statues
- **Largest Buddha**: 17 meters tall
- **Influences**: Indian, Persian, and Greek artistic elements
- **UNESCO**: Listed 2001

### Main Cave Complexes

**Caves 5-6**
- Largest caves
- 17-meter seated Buddha
- Intricate wall carvings
- Most impressive interior decorations

**Caves 16-20 (Tan Yao Five Caves)**
- Oldest section
- Colossal Buddha statues
- Each over 13 meters tall
- Most iconic images

**Cave 1-4**
- Pagoda-style structures
- Multiple levels
- Detailed carvings

**Other Notable Caves**
- Different styles and periods
- Various states of preservation
- Some weathered, others well-preserved

### What to See

**Buddhist Art**
- Various Buddha poses and expressions
- Bodhisattvas and celestial beings
- Detailed clothing and ornamentation
- Flying apsaras (celestial dancers)
- Lotus flowers and decorative patterns

**Architectural Styles**
- Shows cultural exchange via Silk Road
- Indian influences in early caves
- Chinese elements in later work
- Fusion of Eastern and Western art

### Visiting Information

**Duration**
- 2-3 hours minimum
- Full day for detailed exploration

**Getting There**
- 16 km west of Datong
- Bus or taxi (30 minutes)
- Tours available

**Best Time**
- Morning for better light
- Weekdays less crowded
- Spring/autumn for weather

**Facilities**
- Visitor center
- Museum
- Cafes
- Shops

### Photography

- Allowed in most areas
- Some caves prohibit photos
- Best light morning and late afternoon
- Exteriors more photogenic than some interiors

### Practical Tips

- Wear comfortable shoes (lots of walking)
- Bring water
- Audio guides available
- Hire guide for deeper understanding
- Some climbing involved
- Caves can be dark - flashlight helpful

### Nearby

- **Huayan Temple**: In Datong city
- **Nine Dragon Screen**: In Datong city
- **Can combine**: With Hanging Monastery in one long day

### Why Visit

One of China's three most important Buddhist cave complexes (along with Mogao and Longmen), showcasing the incredible artistic achievement of 5th-century China and cultural exchange along the Silk Road.

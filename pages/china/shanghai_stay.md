## Best Areas to Stay in Shanghai

### The Bund & East Nanjing Road
- **Pros**: Historic area, walkable to major sights, great views
- **Cons**: Expensive, touristy
- **Best for**: First-time visitors, luxury travelers

### French Concession
- **Pros**: Charming streets, cafes, boutiques, central location
- **Cons**: Can be pricey
- **Best for**: Culture seekers, foodies

### Jing'an
- **Pros**: Modern, great shopping, convenient metro access
- **Cons**: Less traditional character
- **Best for**: Business travelers, shoppers

### Pudong (Lujiazui)
- **Pros**: Modern hotels, iconic skyline, business district
- **Cons**: Less local character, mainly high-rises
- **Best for**: Business travelers, modern city lovers

### Hongkou
- **Pros**: Authentic neighborhoods, good value
- **Cons**: Further from main tourist sites
- **Best for**: Budget travelers, local experience

## Accommodation Tips

- Book early for Chinese holidays and October Golden Week
- Many international hotel chains represented
- Boutique hotels in French Concession offer unique charm
- Check metro proximity for convenience

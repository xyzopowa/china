## About Hunan Cuisine

Zhangjiajie is in Hunan Province, famous for fiery and intensely flavored cuisine. While not as numbing as Sichuan food (no Sichuan peppercorns), Hunan dishes are often spicier, with fresh chili peppers and bold garlic flavors.

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Spicy**: Fresh red and green chilies (not just dried)
- **Sour**: Pickled vegetables common
- **Salty**: Preserved and cured ingredients
- **Aromatic**: Heavy use of garlic and ginger
- **Fresh Chilies**: Different from Sichuan dried chili

## Signature Local Dishes

### Must-Try Zhangjiajie Specialties

#### Sanxiaguo (三下锅) $$
**The Zhangjiajie Specialty**
- Hearty mixed pot with three ingredients
- Common combinations: pork, tofu, radish OR bacon, intestines, tripe
- Cooked in spicy broth with peppers
- Ultimate comfort food
- **Where**: Hujia Sanxiaguo, anywhere on Xibu Street

#### Tujia Bacon (土家腊肉) $$
**Smoked Bacon**
- Tujia minority specialty
- Smoked over wood fire
- Rich, salty, intense flavor
- Often served with peppers
- Try as standalone or in sanxiaguo

#### Wild Vegetables (野菜) $
**Mountain Vegetables**
- Foraged from surrounding mountains
- Various types depending on season
- Simply stir-fried with garlic
- Unique flavors
- Often featured on local menus

#### Rock Ear Mushroom (岩耳) $$
**Local Delicacy**
- Grows on cliffs in the area
- Chewy texture
- Subtle flavor
- Usually in soups
- Considered health food

#### Zhangjiajie Cold Noodles (凉面) $
**Street Food Classic**
- Rice noodles in spicy sauce
- Peanuts, pickled vegetables
- Refreshing despite being spicy
- Great lunch or snack
- Available throughout town

### Hunan Province Classics

#### Chairman Mao's Red-Braised Pork (毛氏红烧肉) $$
**Hong Shao Rou**
- Mao Zedong's favorite dish
- Fatty pork belly in sweet-savory sauce
- Melt-in-mouth texture
- Rich and indulgent

#### Hunan Steamed Fish Head (剁椒鱼头) $$
**Duo Jiao Yu Tou**
- Fish head with chopped chili peppers
- Intensely spicy and aromatic
- Share with group
- Signature Hunan dish

#### Stir-Fried Pork with Chili (小炒肉) $$
**Xiao Chao Rou**
- Simple but delicious
- Lean pork with fresh green chilies
- Garlic and fermented black beans
- Common home-style dish

**Reality Check**: Food in the park is expensive and basic

**Strategy**:
- **Pack snacks and water**

- Small thermos with hot water
- Plan for 1 simple meal in park if needed

**What's Available**:
- Instant noodles: ¥15-20
- Fried rice: ¥30-40
- Simple stir-fries: ¥40-60
- Bottled water: ¥5-10 (¥2-3 in town)
- Soft drinks: ¥10-15

## Spice Levels

### Managing the Heat

**Key Phrases**:
- **不要辣 (bù yào là)**: "Not spicy" - but may still have some spice
- **微辣 (wēi là)**: "Mildly spicy" - still spicy for most Westerners
- **中辣 (zhōng là)**: "Medium spicy" - VERY spicy
- **重辣 (zhòng là)**: "Extra spicy" - Extreme heat

**Reality**:
- Even "not spicy" may have chili
- "Mild" is still spicy by Western standards
- "Medium" is very hot
- Expect most dishes to have heat

**Coping Strategies**:
- Order rice to calm the heat
- Dairy helps (yogurt drinks)
- Sweet drinks cut spice
- Have tissues ready
- Embrace it - it's authentic Hunan!

## Dietary Considerations

### Vegetarian

**Challenges**:
- Many "vegetable" dishes contain pork or lard
- Meat stock common in soups

**Phrases**:
- **我吃素 (wǒ chī sù)**: "I'm vegetarian"
- **不要肉 (bù yào ròu)**: "No meat"

**Safe Options**:
- Stir-fried vegetables (confirm no meat)
- Tofu dishes (check preparation)
- Rice and noodles
- Eggs
- Wild vegetables

### Vegan

**More Difficult**:
- Tell staff: **我是素食主义者** (wǒ shì sù shí zhǔ yì zhě)
- Avoid sauces (may contain animal products)
- Stick to simple preparations
- Bring snacks

### Allergies

**Key Phrases**:
- **我对...过敏 (wǒ duì...guò mǐn)**: "I'm allergic to..."
- 花生 (huā shēng): peanuts
- 海鲜 (hǎi xiān): seafood
- 鸡蛋 (jī dàn): eggs

**Note**: Cross-contamination common in busy kitchens

## Dining Etiquette

### Ordering
- Point at menu pictures
- Use translation app
- Order multiple dishes to share
- Rice ordered separately
- Tea usually free

### Eating
- Chopsticks only (request fork if needed)
- Lazy Susan on round tables
- Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice
- Bones/scraps on table or plate OK
- Slurping acceptable

### Paying
- Ask for bill: **买单 (mǎi dān)**
- Usually pay at register
- Cash still common
- WeChat/Alipay widely used
- Credit cards hit-or-miss

## Meal Times

- **Dinner**: 5:30-8:00 PM (earlier than Western custom)

## Food Safety

- Stick to busy restaurants (high turnover)
- Hot food safer than cold
- Bottled water only
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid uncooked vegetables unless washed
- Fresh fruit you peel yourself is safe

## About Beijing

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city where ancient history meets modern development. With over 3,000 years of history and numerous UNESCO World Heritage sites, Beijing offers travelers an incredible mix of imperial palaces, ancient temples, and contemporary culture.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (April-May)**: Pleasant weather, blooming flowers
- **Fall (September-October)**: Clear skies, comfortable temperatures
- **Summer**: Hot and humid, peak tourist season
- **Winter**: Cold but less crowded, lower prices

## Getting Around

- **Subway**: Extensive metro system, easy to navigate
- **Taxi/Didi**: Convenient for reaching destinations
- **Bike**: Many bike-sharing options available
- **Walking**: Great for exploring neighborhoods

## Areas to Explore

### Dongcheng District
The heart of historic Beijing, home to the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, and Temple of Heaven. [Learn more →](beijing_dongcheng.html)

### Gubei Water Town
A beautifully restored water town at the foot of the Great Wall, offering traditional architecture and scenic views. [Learn more →](beijing_gubei.html)

### Mutianyu Great Wall
One of the best-preserved and most scenic sections of the Great Wall of China. [Learn more →](beijing_mutianyu.html)

### Chaoyang District
Modern Beijing with contemporary art, shopping, and entertainment. Home to the 798 Art District. [Learn more →](beijing_chaoyang.html)

## Practical Tips

- **Language**: Mandarin Chinese; English limited outside major hotels
- **Currency**: Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB)
- **Transportation Card**: Get a Yikatong card for easy travel
- **Air Quality**: Check pollution levels, especially in winter

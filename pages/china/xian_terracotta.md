## The Terracotta Army (兵马俑)

Discovered in 1974 by local farmers, the Terracotta Army is a collection of over 8,000 life-sized terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. This UNESCO World Heritage site is considered one of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century.

## Visitor Information

- **Location**: 35km northeast of Xi'an city center
- **Admission**: ¥120 (March-November), ¥150 (December-February)
- **Hours**: 8:30 AM - 6:00 PM (summer), 8:30 AM - 5:30 PM (winter)
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours minimum, 4 hours with museum
- **Best Time**: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid peak crowds

## The Three Pits

### Pit 1 (The Main Pit)
- **Size**: 230m x 62m
- **Warriors**: ~6,000 figures (2,000 excavated)
- **Highlights**: Infantry battle formation, impressive scale
- **Must-See**: The most spectacular and complete pit

### Pit 2 (Mixed Formation)
- **Size**: 6,000 square meters
- **Warriors**: ~1,400 figures
- **Highlights**: Cavalry, chariots, archers, and infantry
- **Special**: Close-up viewing of individual warriors

### Pit 3 (Command Post)
- **Size**: Smallest pit, 520 square meters
- **Warriors**: 68 warriors and 1 chariot
- **Highlights**: Military headquarters layout
- **Interest**: Strategic positioning of figures

## Exhibition Halls

### Bronze Chariots and Horses Exhibition Hall
Two ornate bronze chariots discovered near the burial mound:
- **Chariot 1**: Open chariot for reconnaissance
- **Chariot 2**: Covered chariot for passengers
- Incredibly detailed, 50% actual size

### Circular Screen Cinema
Short documentary about the discovery and excavation (English subtitles available)

## Getting There

### Tour Bus (Most Popular)
- **Public Bus 914/915**: From Xi'an Railway Station
- **Duration**: 1-1.5 hours
- **Cost**: ¥7-10
- **Frequency**: Every 15 minutes

### Private Tour
- **Duration**: 45 minutes from city center
- **Cost**: ¥300-500 including guide
- **Convenience**: Hotel pickup, English guide available
- **Recommended**: For first-time visitors

### Taxi/Didi
- **Duration**: 45-60 minutes
- **Cost**: ¥100-150 one way
- **Convenience**: Direct, but negotiate round-trip price

### Organized Tours
Many combine Terracotta Warriors with:
- Huaqing Hot Springs
- Banpo Neolithic Village
- Emperor Qin Shi Huang's Mausoleum

## Tips for Your Visit

### Before You Go
- **Book Tickets Online**: Save time, avoid ticket queues
- **Bring ID**: Passport required for ticket purchase
- **Arrive Early**: Opens at 8:30 AM, arrive by 9:00 AM to beat tour groups
- **Weekdays**: Much less crowded than weekends

### At the Site
- **Visit Order**: Start with Pit 1 (most impressive), then 3, then 2
- **Audio Guide**: ¥30, highly recommended (English available)
- **Guided Tour**: ¥100-200, provides context and history
- **Time Management**: Allocate at least 2-3 hours

### What to Bring
- **Water**: Facilities available but convenient to bring
- **Snacks**: Limited food options on site
- **Comfortable Shoes**: Lots of walking
- **Camera**: Photos allowed (no flash)
- **Weather Gear**: Pits are covered but connecting paths are outdoors

## What Makes Each Warrior Unique

Every warrior has:
- **Individual Facial Features**: No two faces are identical
- **Unique Hand Positions**: Originally held real weapons
- **Distinct Clothing**: Rank indicated by attire and hairstyle
- **Varying Heights**: 175-197cm, representing real army diversity
- **Original Colors**: Traces of paint still being discovered

## Archaeological Significance

- **Scale**: Largest underground army ever discovered
- **Craftsmanship**: Individual molding and detailing
- **Historical Insight**: Information about Qin dynasty military
- **Ongoing Excavation**: New discoveries continue
- **Preservation Challenge**: Exposure to air causes deterioration

## Nearby Attractions

### Emperor Qin Shi Huang's Mausoleum
- **Location**: 1.5km from Terracotta Warriors
- **Status**: Unexcavated main tomb mound
- **Visit**: External viewing only, but interesting historical context

### Huaqing Hot Springs
- **Distance**: 10km from Terracotta Warriors
- **Features**: Historic hot springs, Tang dynasty palace
- **Famous**: Site of the Xi'an Incident (1936)
- **Combination**: Often visited on same day

## Dining

- **On-Site**: Basic restaurant near entrance
- **Nearby**: Small restaurants near parking area
- **Recommendation**: Return to Xi'an for better options
- **Pack Snacks**: Convenient and cost-effective

## Souvenirs

- **Museum Shop**: Officially licensed replicas
- **Quality Range**: From cheap miniatures to museum-quality pieces
- **Prices**: ¥10-10,000+
- **Negotiate**: At outdoor vendors near parking lot
- **Authenticity**: Buy from museum shop for quality assurance

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Arriving Too Late**: Tour groups peak 10 AM - 2 PM
- **Skipping Audio Guide**: Miss important context
- **Rushing**: Need minimum 2-3 hours
- **Wrong Day**: Avoid weekends and Chinese holidays
- **Not Booking Ahead**: Long ticket queues

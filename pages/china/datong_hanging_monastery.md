## Hanging Monastery (Xuankong Si)

One of China's most remarkable architectural achievements - a temple built into a sheer cliff face 50 meters above the ground.

### About the Monastery

- **Built**: 491 AD (Northern Wei Dynasty)
- **Age**: Over 1,500 years old
- **Location**: Cliff of Mount Hengshan
- **Height**: 50 meters above ground
- **Support**: Wooden beams inserted into rock

### What Makes It Special

**Engineering Marvel**
- Appears to defy gravity
- Minimal support structure visible
- Has survived earthquakes
- Protected from flooding by height

**Religious Significance**
- Unique blend: Buddhism, Taoism, Confucianism
- 40 halls and chambers
- Ancient statues and carvings

### The Experience

**Viewing**
- Best viewed from below first
- Photo opportunity
- See full structure

**Visiting Inside**
- Very narrow walkways
- Steep stairs
- Not for those with fear of heights
- Limited capacity
- Can be crowded

**Duration**
- 30-45 minutes inside
- 1-2 hours total including photos

### Practical Information

**Getting There**
- 75 km from Datong city
- 1.5 hours by bus or car
- Tours from Datong recommended

**Tickets**
- Entrance fee
- Optional entry to monastery interior

**Tips**
- Go early to avoid crowds
- Not wheelchair accessible
- Handrails present but narrow passages
- Photography from below free
- Can combine with Yungang Grottoes in one day

### Important Notes

- Weight limits may apply
- Groups may be limited in monastery
- Renovation work possible
- Weather dependent (closed in bad conditions)

### Is It Worth It?

Absolutely - one of China's most unique sites, though the experience inside is brief due to small spaces and crowds.

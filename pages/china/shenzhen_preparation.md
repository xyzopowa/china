## Visa Requirements - IMPORTANT

### Chinese Visa Required

**Most Visitors Need**:
- Valid Chinese visa
- Cannot enter from Hong Kong without proper visa
- Hong Kong visa does NOT allow entry to mainland

### Visa-Free Options (Check Current Rules)

**144-Hour Transit Visa**:
- Available for certain nationalities
- Must arrive by air and depart by air
- Within 144 hours (6 days)
- Usually requires onward ticket to third country
- **Check current eligibility** - rules change

**Other Special Provisions**:
- Check if your country has special arrangements
- Rules change frequently

**Important**: Verify current visa rules before planning trip

## Border Crossings from Hong Kong

### Luohu (罗湖) ★★★★★

**Best For**: First-time visitors, convenience

**Details**:
- Most popular crossing
- MTR East Rail Line from Hong Kong
- Directly connected to Shenzhen Metro
- Open: 6:30 AM - midnight
- Busiest crossing

**Pros**:
- Simple and direct
- Metro connection both sides
- Clear signage in English
- Familiar for tourists

**Cons**:
- Can be very crowded
- Longer queues at peak times

**How It Works**:
1. Take MTR to Luohu Station (HK)
2. Exit Hong Kong immigration
3. Walk across bridge
4. Enter China immigration
5. Continue to Shenzhen Metro

### Futian (福田) ★★★★★

**Best For**: Less crowded alternative

**Details**:
- MTR East Rail Line from Hong Kong
- Directly connected to Shenzhen Metro
- Open: 6:30 AM - 10:30 PM
- Less crowded than Luohu
- Slightly shorter from some HK locations

**Pros**:
- Usually less crowded
- Modern facility
- Central Shenzhen location
- Metro connected

**Cons**:
- Closes earlier than Luohu

### Shenzhen Bay (深圳湾) ★★★

**Best For**: Going to western Shenzhen, quieter crossing

**Details**:
- Bus from Hong Kong
- "Co-location" - both countries' immigration in same building
- Open: 6:30 AM - midnight
- Further from central Shenzhen

**Pros**:
- One-stop immigration (both countries)
- Less crowded
- Good for western Shenzhen

**Cons**:
- Need to take bus
- Further from city center
- Less convenient metro access

### Hong Kong-Shenzhen Western Corridor ★

**Best For**: Drivers only

**Details**:
- For vehicles
- Not commonly used by tourists

## What to Bring for Border Crossing

### Essential Documents
- **Passport** with valid Chinese visa
- **Return ticket** (if on transit visa)
- **Hotel booking** (sometimes checked)
- **Completed arrival card** (given on arrival if needed)

### Prohibited Items
- Check current customs rules
- Drugs strictly prohibited
- Certain foods restricted
- Large amounts of cash must be declared

### Tips
- Arrive with time to spare
- Peak times: weekday mornings and evenings
- Quieter: mid-morning, early afternoon
- Expect 20-60 minute crossing time

## Advance Booking Requirements

### Theme Parks

**Window of the World / Splendid China**
- **Booking**: Can book 1-7 days ahead
- **Walk-up**: Usually available except holidays
- **Official sites or third-party**: Ctrip, Klook
- **Prices**: ¥180-220 per park

**Happy Valley**
- **Booking**: 1-7 days ahead recommended
- **Peak season**: Book earlier
- **Price**: ¥230-280

### DJI Flagship Store Visit
- No booking needed
- Walk-in welcome
- Free entry
- Located in Nanshan District

### Huaqiangbei Electronics Market
- No booking needed
- Open daily
- Just show up and explore

## Accommodation Booking

### When to Book
- **Normal times**: 1-2 weeks ahead
- **Chinese holidays**: 2-4 weeks ahead
- **Trade shows/events**: Book early

## Transportation Booking

### From Hong Kong

**MTR**:
- No advance booking needed
- Buy ticket at station
- Octopus card works to border
- ¥30-40

**Bus to Shenzhen Bay**:
- Can book online or buy on spot
- Buses frequent
- ¥30-40

### Within Shenzhen

**Metro**:
- No booking needed
- Buy ticket or get Shenzhen Tong card
- Very affordable (¥2-10 most rides)

**Taxi/Didi**:
- Book when needed via app
- No advance booking necessary

## Mobile & Internet

### VPN
- **Essential** if you need:
  - Google, Gmail
  - Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp
  - Western news sites
- Download and setup BEFORE arriving
- Test it works

### Phone/Data

**Options**:
1. **Hong Kong SIM with roaming**: Expensive
2. **Chinese SIM card**: Available at border or shops
3. **Portable WiFi**: Rent device
4. **eSIM**: Some services offer China data

**Recommendation**: Get Chinese SIM or eSIM for data

## Payment Methods

### Cashless Society

**WeChat Pay / Alipay**:
- Dominant payment methods
- Can be tricky for foreigners to set up
- Worth trying before trip

**Credit Cards**:
- Accepted at hotels, large stores
- Many small vendors don't accept
- UnionPay more widely accepted than Visa/MC

**Cash**:
- Still useful as backup
- ATMs widely available
- Bring some RMB

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **No visa**: Cannot enter without proper documentation
2. **No VPN**: Cannot access Google, social media
3. **Only credit cards**: Many places don't accept
4. **Peak hour border crossing**: Very crowded
5. **Assuming English everywhere**: Still limited outside expat areas
6. **Forgetting adapter**: China uses different plugs

## Day Trip Logistics from Hong Kong

### Timing Example
- 8:00 AM: Leave Hong Kong
- 9:00 AM: Clear immigration, arrive Shenzhen
- 9:30 AM - 5:00 PM: Explore
- 6:00 PM: Head back to border
- 7:00 PM: Back in Hong Kong

**Feasible**: Yes, but full day
**Worth it**: Depends on interests (electronics, theme parks, experiencing mainland)

## Onboard Dining Overview

Most Yangtze River cruises include meals as part of the package, typically served buffet-style with both Chinese and Western options. The quality and variety depend on your cruise category.

## Meal Inclusions by Cruise Category

### Luxury Cruises (5-Star)

**What's Included**:
- All buffet meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
- Coffee, tea, and juice
- Some snacks
- Captain's welcome and farewell dinners

**Quality**:
- International buffet with variety
- Fresh ingredients
- Some Western comfort foods
- Professional presentation
- À la carte dining (some ships)

**Not Included**:
- Alcoholic beverages
- Premium coffee drinks
- Specialty restaurants (if available)
- Room service (usually extra)

### Standard Cruises (4-Star)

**What's Included**:
- Buffet breakfast, lunch, dinner
- Basic beverages with meals
- Welcome dinner

**Quality**:
- Mostly Chinese cuisine
- Some Western options
- Good variety
- Fresh and well-prepared

**Not Included**:
- Alcohol
- Specialty drinks
- Snacks between meals

### Budget Cruises (3-Star)

**What's Included**:
- Basic Chinese meals
- Limited beverage options

**Quality**:
- Simple Chinese dishes
- Minimal Western options
- Less variety
- Adequate but not exceptional

## Dining Experience

### Breakfast (Usually 7:00-9:00 AM)

**Chinese Options**:
- Congee (rice porridge)
- Steamed buns (baozi)
- Fried dough sticks (youtiao)
- Pickled vegetables
- Noodles
- Hard-boiled eggs

**Western Options** (4-5 star ships):
- Scrambled eggs
- Toast and pastries
- Cereal
- Fruit
- Coffee and juice

### Lunch (Usually 12:00-2:00 PM)

**Typical Offerings**:
- Hot dishes (5-15 options depending on ship)
- Rice and noodles
- Vegetables
- Meat and fish dishes
- Soup
- Fresh fruit
- Salad bar (on better ships)

**Style**: Buffet, self-serve

### Dinner (Usually 6:30-8:30 PM)

**Similar to Lunch but Often**:
- More elaborate dishes
- Special themed nights (some ships)
- Captain's dinner (formal occasion)
- Slightly larger selection

### Snacks and Treats

**Premium Ships**:
- Afternoon tea service
- Fruit available
- Light snacks
- Late-night options

**Standard Ships**:
- Limited snacking options
- Bring your own if desired

## Special Meals

### Captain's Welcome Dinner
- More formal occasion
- Special menu
- Traditional toasts
- Opportunity to meet fellow travelers

### Farewell Dinner
- Celebration of the journey
- Often includes entertainment
- Group photos
- Tipping ceremony

## Dietary Considerations

### Special Diets

**Vegetarian**:
- Usually easy to accommodate
- Notify cruise in advance
- Buffets have vegetable dishes
- May be limited variety

**Vegan**:
- More challenging
- Definitely notify in advance
- Bring supplemental snacks

**Allergies**:
- Notify cruise company when booking
- Language barrier can be an issue
- Bring translation card in Chinese
- Stick to simple, identifiable foods

**Western Food Preference**:
- Better on 5-star ships
- Limited on budget cruises
- Consider bringing familiar snacks

## Beverages

### Included (Usually)
- Tea and coffee at meals
- Water (check if bottled or filtered)
- Juice at breakfast

### Available for Purchase
- **Beer**: ¥20-40 per bottle
- **Wine**: ¥100-300+ per bottle
- **Cocktails**: ¥40-80
- **Soft drinks**: ¥10-20
- **Premium coffee**: ¥20-30

### Bar Service
- Most ships have a bar
- Prices higher than on land
- Can bring own alcohol (check policy)
- Some ships offer beverage packages

## Dining Tips

1. **Don't Miss Meals**: Limited alternative options
2. **Try Local Dishes**: Sichuan and Chongqing specialties
3. **Buffet Strategy**: Survey all options before filling plate
4. **Bring Snacks**: Especially if picky eater
5. **Stay Hydrated**: Air conditioning can be drying
6. **Ask About Ingredients**: If allergies are a concern
7. **Attend Captain's Dinner**: Cultural experience and fun

## Regional Specialties to Try

Even on cruises, you may encounter regional Sichuan/Chongqing flavors:

### Sichuan/Chongqing Flavors
- **Mapo Tofu**: Spicy tofu in chili oil
- **Kung Pao Chicken**: Sweet and spicy
- **Twice-Cooked Pork**: Rich and flavorful
- **Dan Dan Noodles**: Spicy noodles
- **Sichuan Peppercorns**: Numbing spice

### Yangtze River Specialties
- **River Fish**: Fresh catch from the Yangtze
- **Three Gorges Fish**: Local preparation
- **River Shrimp**: Small, sweet shrimp

### Hubei Province Dishes
- **Wuchang Fish**: Famous Yangtze fish
- **Hot Dry Noodles** (Re Gan Mian): Yichang/Wuhan specialty

## Dining Etiquette

- Cruise dining is casual
- Captain's dinner slightly dressier (smart casual)
- Buffet style: no need to wait to be seated
- Take what you'll eat; avoid waste
- Tipping not required at meals (end-of-cruise tip covers all)

## What to Bring

- **Hand sanitizer**: Use before buffet
- **Favorite snacks**: For between meals
- **Water bottle**: Stay hydrated
- **Instant coffee**: If particular about coffee
- **Seasickness remedies**: Just in case

## Meal Alternatives

### On Shore Excursions
- Sometimes lunch is at a local restaurant
- Included in excursion
- Opportunity to try local food
- Usually Chinese style

### Skipping Meals
- No refund if you skip meals
- Nearby dining not usually an option while sailing
- Plan accordingly

## Food Safety

- **Luxury/Standard Ships**: Generally very safe
- **Stick to Cooked Foods**: Always safer
- **Bottled Water**: Use for drinking
- **Wash Hands**: Before eating
- **Hot Foods**: Better than cold items

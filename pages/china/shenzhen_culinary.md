## About Shenzhen's Food Scene

Shenzhen's food culture is unique in China - as a young city of migrants from across the country, it offers incredible diversity. You'll find authentic cuisine from every Chinese province, plus extensive international options thanks to the large expat community. While not as refined as Guangzhou's Cantonese tradition, Shenzhen compensates with variety and innovation.

## Food Characteristics

**Diverse Not Traditional**:
- No single "Shenzhen cuisine"
- Migrants brought regional foods
- Cantonese base (Guangdong Province)
- Fusion and innovation
- International influence
- Less authentic, more accessible

**Key Features**:
- Regional Chinese cuisine representation
- Fresh seafood (coastal city)
- Cantonese influence (lighter flavors)
- International restaurants (expat demand)
- Modern dining scene
- Food courts in malls
- Delivery culture

## Dietary Considerations

### Vegetarian

**Easier than Northern China**:
- Cantonese uses less meat in some dishes
- Buddhist vegetarian restaurants exist
- International restaurants have options

**Challenges**:
- Still a meat-centric culture
- Confirm no meat stock
- Oyster sauce common

**Phrases**:
- 我吃素 (wǒ chī sù): I'm vegetarian
- 不要肉 (bù yào ròu): No meat

### Vegan

**More Challenging**:
- Eggs and dairy less common than meat
- Specify clearly
- International restaurants better options

### Allergies

**Common Allergens**:
- Seafood (everywhere in coastal city)
- Peanuts
- Soy

**Important**:
- 我对...过敏 (wǒ duì...guò mǐn): I'm allergic to...
- Carry allergy card in Chinese
- Cross-contamination common

### Spice Sensitivity

**Good News**:
- Cantonese food not spicy
- Can avoid Sichuan/Hunan restaurants
- Many mild options

**If Ordering Spicy Cuisine**:
- 不要辣 (bù yào là): Not spicy
- May still have some heat

## Dining Etiquette

### Ordering
- Share multiple dishes
- Rice ordered separately
- Point at pictures helps
- Use translation app

### Eating
- Chopsticks only (usually)
- Request fork if needed
- Slurping OK
- Bones on table acceptable

### Paying
- "买单" (mǎi dān): Check please
- Pay at register or table
- Mobile payment dominant
- Cash works
- Credit cards at nicer places

### Tipping
- **Not expected**
- Service charge sometimes included
- No need to tip

## Practical Information

### Meal Times

- **Breakfast**: 7:00-9:00 AM (though many eat later)
- **Lunch**: 11:30 AM - 2:00 PM
- **Dinner**: 5:30 PM - 9:00 PM

**Note**: Some restaurants close between lunch and dinner

### Delivery Culture

**Extremely Popular**:
- Meituan, Ele.me apps
- Fast and cheap
- Requires Chinese phone number/app
- Hotels can help order

### Food Safety

- **Generally very safe**
- Stick to busy restaurants
- Food courts very reliable
- Bottled water
- Cooked food safer than raw

## Language Tips

### Point and Order
- Use photos on menus
- Point at what others are eating
- Show pictures on phone

### Basic Phrases
- This: 这个 (zhè ge) - point at menu
- Same: 一样 (yí yàng) - point at another table
- Delicious: 好吃 (hǎo chī)
- Too spicy: 太辣了 (tài là le)

## Special Notes

- Shenzhen more international than most Chinese cities
- English menus increasingly common (Shekou, Coco Park)
- Quality generally good
- Food delivery culture means you can try variety from hotel
- Cash becoming rare - mobile payment or cards
- Portions often large - order conservatively

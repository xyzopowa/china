## Mt. Huashan (华山)

Mt. Huashan is one of China's Five Great Mountains and is renowned for having some of the most dangerous hiking trails in the world. Located 120km east of Xi'an, it offers spectacular cliff-side paths, temples, and panoramic views.

## Visitor Information

- **Location**: 120km from Xi'an (2-hour drive)
- **Admission**: ¥160 (March-November), ¥100 (December-February)
- **Cable Cars**: ¥150 round-trip (West Peak), ¥140 (North Peak)
- **Hours**: 24 hours (cable cars 7 AM - 7 PM)
- **Best Time**: April-October, avoid rain

## The Five Peaks

1. **North Peak (Cloud Terrace, 1,614m)**: Lowest, easiest access
2. **East Peak (Facing Sun, 2,090m)**: Sunrise viewing
3. **South Peak (Falling Goose, 2,154m)**: Highest peak
4. **West Peak (Lotus, 2,082m)**: Most beautiful  
5. **Center Peak (Jade Maiden, 2,037m)**: Quietest

## Hiking Routes

### Option 1: Cable Car Up & Down (Easiest)
- **Duration**: 5-6 hours total
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Best For**: Limited time, less fit visitors
- **Route**: West Peak cable car, visit main peaks, return same way

### Option 2: Night Hike for Sunrise
- **Duration**: 4-5 hours up, 3-4 hours down
- **Difficulty**: Challenging
- **Start**: Midnight from North Peak trail
- **Best For**: Adventure seekers, photographers

### Option 3: Full Circuit
- **Duration**: 8-12 hours
- **Difficulty**: Very challenging
- **Route**: Hike up, visit all peaks, cable car down
- **Best For**: Experienced hikers

## Famous Attractions

### Plank Walk (长空栈道)
- **Danger Level**: Extreme
- **Height**: Hundreds of meters above ground
- **Safety**: Harnesses provided
- **Cost**: ¥30 rental fee
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes

### Chess Pavilion
Legendary site where a Daoist master played chess with an emperor

### Changkong Zhandao (Aerial Walkway)
Narrow plank path along cliff face

## What to Bring

- **Sturdy Shoes**: Essential for rocky, steep paths
- **Gloves**: Helpful for chain sections  
- **Water & Snacks**: Expensive on mountain
- **Warm Layers**: Temperature drops significantly at peaks
- **Flashlight**: If hiking at night
- **Rain Gear**: Weather changes quickly

## Getting There

### Tour Bus
- **From Xi'an**: Morning departure, evening return
- **Cost**: ¥120-200 including transport
- **Convenience**: Hassle-free option

### Public Transportation
1. **Train**: Xi'an to Huashan North Station (30-40 min)
2. **Local Bus**: From station to mountain (20 min)
- **Total Cost**: ¥50-80
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours total

### Private Car/Taxi
- **Duration**: 2 hours from Xi'an  
- **Cost**: ¥600-800 round-trip
- **Best For**: Groups, flexibility

## Tips

- **Fitness Required**: Even with cable car, significant climbing involved
- **Start Early**: Avoid crowds and heat
- **Check Weather**: Do not attempt in rain (very dangerous)
- **Insurance**: Consider travel insurance for adventure activities
- **Acrophobia**: Not suitable for those with fear of heights

## Accommodation

### On Mountain
- **Options**: Basic guesthouses at various peaks
- **Cost**: ¥200-400 (very basic)
- **Purpose**: For sunrise viewing
- **Book Ahead**: Limited capacity

### At Base
- **Huashan Town**: Budget to mid-range hotels
- **Better Value**: More comfortable than mountain lodging

## Dining

- **On Mountain**: Limited, expensive options
- **Bring Food**: Most economical choice
- **At Base**: Restaurants in Huashan town

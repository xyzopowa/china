## Chaoyang District (朝阳区)

Chaoyang is Beijing's largest and most international district, representing the modern face of China's capital. It's home to most foreign embassies, international businesses, contemporary art scenes, and upscale shopping.

## Top Attractions

### 798 Art District (798艺术区)

![798 Art District](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1581895382819-0f8c63c0e3c2?w=1200&q=80)
*Industrial-chic art galleries in former factory buildings*

**Duration**: 2-3 hours  
**Admission**: Free (individual galleries may charge)

A thriving contemporary art community housed in decommissioned military factories from the 1950s. The district features galleries, studios, cafes, and boutiques.

**Highlights**:
- **Galleries**: Rotating exhibitions from Chinese and international artists
- **Street Art**: Impressive murals and installations
- **Design Shops**: Unique home goods and fashion
- **Cafes**: Trendy spots for coffee and lunch
- **Photography**: Industrial-chic architecture

**Best Time**: Weekdays for quieter browsing, weekends for events and openings

### Sanlitun (三里屯)

![Sanlitun District](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1555993539-1732b0258235?w=1200&q=80)
*Modern Sanlitun shopping and entertainment district*

**Duration**: 2-4 hours  
**Best For**: Shopping, dining, nightlife

Beijing's trendiest neighborhood with international brands, restaurants, and entertainment.

**Areas**:
- **Taikoo Li**: Upscale outdoor shopping complex
- **Sanlitun Village**: Mixed-use development
- **Bar Street**: Popular nightlife destination

### CBD (Central Business District)
**Duration**: 1-2 hours  
**Best For**: Architecture, city views

Beijing's modern skyline with impressive skyscrapers.

**Highlights**:
- **CCTV Headquarters**: Iconic "Big Pants" building
- **China World Trade Center**: Shopping and observation deck
- **Guomao**: Major business hub

### Chaoyang Park (朝阳公园)
**Duration**: 1-2 hours  
**Admission**: ¥5

Beijing's largest park offering green space, lakes, and recreational activities.

**Activities**:
- Boating
- Cycling
- Picnicking
- Seasonal festivals and events

## Shopping

### Luxury Shopping
- **SKP**: China's highest-grossing luxury mall
- **China World Mall**: International luxury brands
- **Parkview Green**: Eco-friendly luxury complex

### Markets and Malls
- **Sanlitun Taikoo Li**: Mix of international brands
- **Silk Street Market**: Famous for bargaining (tourist-oriented)
- **Yashow Market**: Clothing and accessories

## Dining

### International Cuisine
Chaoyang has Beijing's best selection of international restaurants:
- **Japanese**: Abundant high-quality options
- **Korean**: Large Korean community, authentic food
- **Western**: American, Italian, French cuisine
- **Fusion**: Creative East-meets-West concepts

### Notable Restaurants
- **TRB Hutong**: Fine dining in historic setting
- **Jing Yaa Tang**: Upscale Peking Duck
- **The Georg**: Modern European
- **Home Plate BBQ**: American-style barbecue

## Nightlife

### Bar Streets
- **Sanlitun Bar Street**: Mix of local and expat venues
- **Gongti (Workers Stadium)**: Club district
- **Nali Patio**: Sophisticated bars and restaurants

### Clubs
- **Mix Club**: Electronic music
- **Vics**: Live music venue
- **Dada**: Underground electronic scene

## Getting There

### Subway
- **Line 10**: Serves 798 Art District and eastern Chaoyang
- **Line 1**: Connects to CBD (Guomao Station)
- **Line 2**: Convenient for Sanlitun and Gongti
- **Line 14**: New line serving eastern areas

### Taxi/Didi
Easy to hail taxis throughout the district

## Tips

- **Language**: More English spoken here than other districts
- **Prices**: Generally higher than other areas of Beijing
- **Walking**: District is large; plan transportation between areas
- **Weekends**: 798 Art District busiest on weekends
- **Evening**: Sanlitun comes alive after dark

## Recommended Itinerary

### Half Day (Morning/Afternoon)
1. 798 Art District (2-3 hours)
2. Lunch at 798 or transit to Sanlitun
3. Sanlitun shopping and cafes (2 hours)

### Full Day
1. Morning: 798 Art District
2. Lunch: Restaurant in 798
3. Afternoon: Sanlitun shopping
4. Evening: Dinner and bars in Sanlitun

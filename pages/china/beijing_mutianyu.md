## Mutianyu Great Wall (慕田峪长城)

Mutianyu is one of the best-preserved sections of the Great Wall of China, offering stunning mountain views and a more comfortable visitor experience than the crowded Badaling section.

## Why Choose Mutianyu

- **Less Crowded**: Fewer tourists than Badaling
- **Well-Preserved**: Extensive restoration maintaining authentic character
- **Beautiful Scenery**: Surrounded by lush forest
- **Modern Facilities**: Cable car, chairlift, and toboggan options
- **Family-Friendly**: Easier access with transportation options

## Visitor Information

- **Location**: Huairou District, 70km from Beijing
- **Admission**: ¥40 (March-November), ¥35 (December-February)
- **Hours**: 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM (summer), 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM (winter)
- **Duration**: 3-4 hours typical visit
- **Best Time**: Spring and Fall, early morning or late afternoon

## Getting Up the Wall

### Cable Car
- **Cost**: ¥100 round trip, ¥80 one way
- **Duration**: 5 minutes
- **Best For**: Quick access, saving energy

### Chairlift
- **Cost**: ¥100 round trip, ¥80 one way
- **Duration**: Slightly longer than cable car
- **Experience**: Open-air ride with great views

### Hiking
- **Cost**: Free (just entrance ticket)
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes climb
- **Best For**: Fitness enthusiasts, authentic experience

## Coming Down

### Toboggan Slide
- **Cost**: ¥100 (if not included in lift combo)
- **Experience**: Fun 1.5km slide down the mountain
- **Popular**: Booking in advance recommended
- **Note**: Minimum age restrictions apply

### Walk Down
- Free and good exercise
- Takes about 40 minutes

## Hiking Routes

### Tower 6 to Tower 23 (Most Popular)
- **Distance**: About 2.6km
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours
- **Difficulty**: Moderate, some steep sections
- **Highlights**: Best restored sections, amazing views

### Extended Routes
For more adventurous hikers, you can explore beyond Tower 23 to less-visited sections.

## Getting There

### Tour Bus
- **Duration**: 2 hours from Beijing
- **Cost**: ¥120-150 including transport and ticket
- **Convenient**: Hotel pickup available

### Public Transportation
1. Subway Line 2 to Dongzhimen Station
2. Bus 916 Express to Huairou Bus Station
3. Local bus or taxi to Mutianyu
- **Duration**: 3+ hours
- **Cost**: Budget-friendly but time-consuming

### Private Car/Taxi
- **Duration**: 1.5-2 hours
- **Cost**: ¥600-800 round trip
- **Best For**: Groups, families, convenience

### Didi (Chinese Uber)
- More affordable than taxi
- Reliable service
- Easy to book via app

## What to Bring

- **Comfortable Shoes**: Essential for walking on uneven stones
- **Water**: Stay hydrated
- **Snacks**: Limited options on the wall
- **Sunscreen**: No shade on the wall
- **Jacket**: Can be windy and cool at elevation
- **Camera**: Incredible photo opportunities

## Tips

- **Arrive Early**: Best light and fewer crowds before 10 AM
- **Weekdays**: Much less crowded than weekends
- **Combo Tickets**: Save money with cable car/toboggan combinations
- **Weather**: Check forecast, avoid rainy days (slippery)
- **Crowds**: Chinese holidays are extremely busy

## Dining

- **On-Site Restaurant**: Basic options at entrance
- **Bring Food**: Allowed to bring snacks and drinks
- **Nearby**: Few restaurants in surrounding area
- **Return to Beijing**: More dining options

## Nearby Attractions

- **Hongluo Temple**: Ancient Buddhist temple
- **Rainbow Trout Farm**: Local specialty fish restaurants
- **Apple Orchards**: Seasonal fruit picking (fall)

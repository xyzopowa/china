## Beijing Cuisine

Beijing's culinary scene offers a rich tapestry of flavors, from imperial cuisine to street food delights.

### Signature Dishes

#### Peking Duck (Beijing Kaoya)
The most famous Beijing dish - crispy skinned duck served with thin pancakes, scallions, and sweet bean sauce.

#### Jiaozi (Dumplings)
Traditional northern-style dumplings filled with pork and vegetables.

#### Zhajiangmian
Hand-pulled noodles with savory soybean paste sauce, a local favorite.

### Street Food

- **Jianbing**: Savory crepes with egg, crispy wonton, scallions, and sauces
- **Tanghulu**: Candied fruit skewers, especially hawthorn berries
- **Baozi**: Steamed buns with various fillings

### Where to Eat

#### Traditional Restaurants
Look for established Peking duck restaurants in Dongcheng District.

#### Night Markets
Experience authentic street food at Wangfujing Snack Street or Donghuamen Night Market.

#### Hutong Eateries
Small family-run restaurants in hutongs offer authentic home-style cooking.

### Dining Tips

- Reservations recommended for famous restaurants
- Learn basic food phrases in Mandarin
- Be adventurous but know your spice tolerance
- Tea houses offer traditional Beijing snacks

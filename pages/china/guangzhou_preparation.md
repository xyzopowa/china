## Advance Booking Requirements

### What to Book Ahead

**Transportation**
- **High-speed train from Hong Kong**: Book 1-2 weeks ahead if traveling on weekend/holiday
- **Domestic flights**: Book for best prices, not mandatory
- **Pearl River cruise**: Can book 1-3 days ahead or walk up (low season)

**Accommodation**
- **Peak seasons**: Book 1-2 weeks ahead
- **Canton Fair times** (April, October): Book 1-2 months ahead
- **Normal times**: 3-7 days ahead sufficient

### What Doesn't Require Booking

Most Guangzhou attractions don't require advance booking:
- Chen Clan Ancestral Hall (walk-up OK)
- Canton Tower (can book same day online)
- Museums (free entry, walk-in)
- Temple of the Six Banyan Trees
- Shamian Island (public area)
- Most restaurants (except famous ones at peak times)

## Canton Fair Alert

### What It Is
- China Import and Export Fair
- Held **twice yearly**: April and October
- Massive trade show attracting 200,000+ visitors

### Impact on Travel
- **Hotels**: Prices triple, book months ahead
- **Trains**: Heavily booked
- **Restaurants**: Busier than usual
- **Attractions**: Less affected (visitors mostly business travelers)

**Dates** (check annually):
- Spring session: Mid-April to early May
- Autumn session: Mid-October to early November

**Recommendation**: Avoid these periods unless attending the fair

## Ticket Booking Information

### Canton Tower (广州塔)

**Advance Booking**: Recommended but not required

**Options**:
- Book online: Official website or travel platforms (Ctrip, Klook)
- Walk-up tickets: Available but may have queues
- Same-day online: Usually fine

**Ticket Types**:
- **108F Observation Deck**: ¥150
- **433m Top Deck + Bubble Tram**: ¥298
- **Various combinations**: Available
- **Ferris Wheel**: Extra ¥130
- **Sky Drop**: ¥60-100

**When to Book**: 1-3 days ahead for convenience, or same day

### Pearl River Night Cruise (珠江夜游)

**Advance Booking**: Optional

**Options**:
- **Online**: 1-7 days ahead (Ctrip, Klook, official sites)
- **Pier**: Walk-up tickets usually available
- **Hotel**: Many hotels can arrange

**Ticket Types**:
- Standard cruise: ¥60-88 (1 hour)
- Luxury cruise: ¥128-188 (better boat, 1-1.5 hours)
- VIP options: ¥200+

**Departure Points**:
- Dashatou Pier (most common)
- Tianzi Pier
- West Dike Pier

**Times**: Multiple departures 7:00 PM - 9:30 PM

### Chen Clan Ancestral Hall (陈家祠)

**Advance Booking**: Not required

- **Entry Fee**: ¥10
- **Purchase**: On-site
- **Hours**: 8:30 AM - 5:30 PM
- **Rarely crowded**: Walk-up fine

### Museums (Free Admission)

**Guangdong Museum**
- Free with passport/ID
- Can register online to skip queue
- Walk-in also possible

**Guangzhou Museum** (in Yuexiu Park)
- ¥10 entrance fee
- Buy on-site

**Museum of the Western Han Dynasty Mausoleum of the Nanyue King**
- ¥12 entrance fee
- Buy on-site

## Transportation Booking

### High-Speed Train

**Hong Kong → Guangzhou**
- Route: West Kowloon → Guangzhou South (48 min)
- **Book**: 1-2 weeks ahead for weekends/holidays
- **Platforms**: 12306.cn (Chinese), Trip.com (English)
- **Price**: ¥215-247 second class

**Domestic Routes**
- Guangzhou ↔ Shenzhen: Easy to get tickets
- Guangzhou ↔ Other cities: Book 1-2 weeks ahead

### Flights

**Baiyun Airport (CAN)**
- Book 2-4 weeks ahead for best prices
- Last-minute often available but pricier
- Check airport express train schedule

### Airport Transfer

**Metro**: No booking needed
- Line 3 or Airport Express
- ¥7-15 to city center
- 40-60 minutes

**Taxi/Didi**: Book on arrival or pre-book through hotel

## Restaurant Reservations

### When You Need Reservations

**Famous Dim Sum Restaurants**:
- **Panxi Restaurant**: Reserve 1-3 days ahead
- **Guangzhou Restaurant**: Reserve for weekend mornings
- **Bingsheng Mansion**: Weekends get busy

**How to Reserve**:
- Dianping app (Chinese)
- Hotel concierge
- Call directly (Chinese needed)

### When You Don't Need Reservations

- Most street food
- Casual restaurants
- Less famous dim sum spots
- Lunch at random restaurants

**Strategy**: Walk-ins work for most places, or eat slightly off-peak hours

## Accommodation Booking

### Best Areas to Stay

**Tianhe District** (天河区)
- Modern, upscale
- Shopping and business district
- Good metro connections
- Canton Tower nearby

**Shamian Island/Liwan** (沙面/荔湾)
- Historical charm
- European architecture
- Near Shangxiajiu shopping street
- More atmospheric

**Yuexiu District** (越秀区)
- Central location
- Near Beijing Road shopping
- Mix of old and new
- Good value

**Pearl River New City** (珠江新城)
- Modern, expensive
- Financial district
- Canton Tower, Opera House
- International hotels

### Booking Timeline

- **Canton Fair periods**: 1-2 months ahead minimum
- **Chinese holidays**: 2-4 weeks ahead
- **Normal times**: 1 week ahead
- **Last minute**: Usually fine except during events

### Budget Estimates

- **Budget**: ¥150-300/night (hostels, budget hotels)
- **Mid-range**: ¥300-600/night (3-4 star)
- **Upscale**: ¥600-1,500/night (4-5 star international)
- **Luxury**: ¥1,500+/night (top hotels)

## What to Pack

### Clothing
- **Light, breathable fabrics**: Heat and humidity
- **Rain jacket or umbrella**: Frequent rain
- **Comfortable walking shoes**: Lots of walking
- **Layers**: Air conditioning can be strong indoors
- **Sun protection**: Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen

### Essentials
- **Passport**: Required for hotel check-in
- **Payment**: Cash (some places), credit card, or mobile pay
- **Translation app**: Limited English
- **Portable charger**: Heavy phone use for navigation
- **Adapter**: Type A/I/G plugs
- **Medications**: Stomach remedies, personal meds

### Tech
- **VPN**: If you need to access blocked sites
- **Download offline maps**: Google Maps may not work well
- **WeChat**: Useful for many services
- **Dianping app**: For restaurant reviews and bookings

## Money Matters

### Cash vs. Card
- **Mobile payment** (WeChat/Alipay) most common
- **Cash**: Still useful, carry some
- **Credit cards**: Accepted at hotels, major stores
- **ATMs**: Widely available

### Budget Planning

**Daily Budget Estimates**:
- **Budget traveler**: ¥200-400/day (food, local transport, basic sights)
- **Mid-range**: ¥500-1,000/day (nicer meals, taxis, paid attractions)
- **Comfort**: ¥1,000-2,000/day (better dining, more comfort)

**Excludes**: Accommodation, long-distance transport

## Language Preparation

### Useful Apps
- **Translation**: Google Translate (download offline Chinese)
- **Pleco**: Chinese dictionary with camera function
- **Dianping**: Restaurant reviews (Chinese)
- **Didi**: Ride-hailing (English version available)

### Key Phrases (Mandarin/Cantonese differ)
- **Hello**: 你好 (nǐ hǎo) / 你好 (néih hóu)
- **Thank you**: 谢谢 (xiè xie) / 唔該 (m̀h gōi)
- **How much**: 多少钱 (duō shao qián) / 幾多錢 (géi dō chín)

### Restaurant Cards
- Download or print common dietary restrictions in Chinese
- "No spicy", "vegetarian", "allergy to..." etc.

## Timing Your Visit

### Best Times
- **Late March-April**: Warm, flowers blooming (avoid Canton Fair)
- **October-November**: Comfortable, dry (avoid Canton Fair)
- **December-February**: Mild winter, fewer tourists

### Times to Avoid
- **Canton Fair**: Mid-April to early May, mid-October to early November
- **Chinese New Year**: January/February (exact dates vary)
- **Summer (July-August)**: Very hot and humid

## Important Notes

- Most attractions close by 5:30-6:00 PM
- Many restaurants close 2:30-5:00 PM (between meals)
- Monday often closing day for museums
- English signage improving but still limited
- Locals are helpful but language barrier significant
- Guangzhou is safe for travelers
- Street food is generally safe from reputable vendors

## About Xi'an (西安)

Xi'an, formerly known as Chang'an, served as the capital for 13 dynasties over 1,100 years. This ancient city is one of the oldest in China and was the eastern terminus of the Silk Road. Today, it perfectly blends its rich historical heritage with modern development.

## Why Visit Xi'an

- **Terracotta Warriors**: One of the world's greatest archaeological discoveries
- **Ancient City Wall**: Best-preserved city fortifications in China
- **Silk Road History**: Starting point of the ancient trade route
- **Muslim Quarter**: Vibrant cultural and culinary experience
- **Mt. Huashan**: One of China's Five Great Mountains

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (March-May)**: Pleasant weather, flowers blooming
- **Fall (September-November)**: Clear skies, comfortable temperatures
- **Summer**: Hot but manageable, peak season
- **Winter**: Cold but fewer crowds, lower prices

## Getting There

### By Air
- **Xi'an Xianyang International Airport**: Well-connected to major Chinese and international cities
- **Airport Express**: Fast connection to city center
- **Distance**: 47km from city center, 1 hour by shuttle

### By Train
- **High-Speed Rail**: From Beijing (4.5 hours), Shanghai (6 hours), Chengdu (3.5 hours)
- **Xi'an Railway Station**: City center location
- **Xi'an North Railway Station**: High-speed rail hub

## Main Attractions

### Terracotta Army
The crown jewel of Xi'an, featuring thousands of life-sized warrior statues guarding Emperor Qin Shi Huang's tomb. [Learn more →](xian_terracotta.html)

### Mt. Huashan
One of China's Five Sacred Mountains, famous for its steep trails and breathtaking views. [Learn more →](xian_huashan.html)

### Xi'an City
Explore the ancient city walls, Muslim Quarter, and numerous historical sites within the city. [Learn more →](xian_city.html)

## Getting Around

- **Subway**: Modern metro system, easy to navigate
- **City Wall Bike**: Rent bikes to ride atop the ancient wall
- **Taxi/Didi**: Convenient and affordable
- **Walking**: Old city center is pedestrian-friendly

## Food Specialties

Xi'an is famous for its unique cuisine, influenced by both Chinese and Middle Eastern flavors:

- **Roujiamo (肉夹馍)**: Chinese "hamburger"
- **Biangbiang Noodles**: Wide, hand-pulled noodles
- **Yangrou Paomo (羊肉泡馍)**: Lamb soup with bread
- **Liangpi (凉皮)**: Cold skin noodles
- **Persimmon Cakes**: Sweet local dessert

## Practical Information

- **Language**: Mandarin Chinese; limited English
- **Currency**: Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB)
- **Climate**: Continental, hot summers, cold winters
- **Duration**: 2-4 days recommended for main sights

## Accommodation Areas

### Inside City Wall
- **Pros**: Historic atmosphere, walking to main sights
- **Cons**: Can be touristy, limited nightlife
- **Best For**: First-time visitors, history enthusiasts

### Muslim Quarter Area
- **Pros**: Great food, vibrant atmosphere
- **Cons**: Can be noisy, crowded
- **Best For**: Foodies, cultural experience

### Outside City Wall (Modern Districts)
- **Pros**: Modern amenities, shopping, restaurants
- **Cons**: Further from historic sites
- **Best For**: Longer stays, business travelers

## Booking Your Cruise

### When to Book

- **Recommended**: 2-3 months in advance
- **Peak Season** (April-May, September-October): Book 3-4 months ahead
- **Last Minute**: Sometimes available but limited cabin choice

### What's Typically Included

- Cabin accommodation
- Most meals (buffet style)
- Basic shore excursions
- Onboard entertainment
- English-speaking guide

### What's Usually Extra

- Premium cabin upgrades
- Alcoholic beverages
- Optional excursions
- Tipping for crew and guides
- Personal expenses

## Cruise Categories

### Luxury Cruises ($$$$)
- 5-star vessels
- Spacious cabins with balconies
- International cuisine
- Premium shore excursions
- English-speaking staff
- **Examples**: Victoria Cruises, Century Cruises premium ships

### Standard Cruises ($$$)
- 4-star vessels
- Comfortable cabins
- Good meals and service
- Basic shore excursions
- **Examples**: Yangtze Gold series, President series

### Budget Cruises ($$)
- 3-star vessels
- Basic cabins
- Chinese-style meals
- Limited English service
- **Note**: Consider carefully, as you'll spend several days onboard

## Booking Options

### Through Travel Agencies
- Most reliable for international travelers
- Packages often include transfers
- Better English support
- Can combine with other China travel

### Direct Booking
- Cruise company websites
- May offer better prices
- Requires more Chinese language ability
- Self-arrange transfers

### Recommended Booking Period
- **3-4 months ahead**: Best selection and prices
- **1-2 months ahead**: Still good options
- **Last minute**: Risk of limited availability

## What to Bring

### Essential Items
- Passport (required for boarding)
- Cruise booking confirmation
- Comfortable walking shoes for excursions
- Light jacket or sweater (air conditioning onboard)
- Sunscreen and hat
- Camera and binoculars
- Motion sickness medication (if prone to seasickness)

### Clothing
- Layers for varying temperatures
- Rain jacket or umbrella
- Casual wear for most occasions
- Slightly dressier outfit for captain's dinner (optional)

### Optional
- Swimsuit (some luxury ships have pools)
- Books or entertainment for relaxing
- Power adapter for charging devices

## Embarkation

### Check-in Process
- Arrive at the dock 2-3 hours before departure
- Bring passport and booking confirmation
- Cabin assignments confirmed at check-in
- Safety briefing required before departure

### Departure Times
- Usually late afternoon/evening
- Allows time for travel to the dock
- First night often spent docked or on calm waters

## Important Notes

- Most cruises depart from Chongqing's Chaotianmen Dock
- Yichang departures from Maoping or Sandouping (near Three Gorges Dam)
- Check exact departure location when booking
- Plan transportation to/from the dock in advance
- Keep valuables in cabin safe
- Wi-Fi often limited or unavailable on the river

## Typical Itinerary Timeline

### Downstream (Chongqing → Yichang)
- **Day 1**: Board in Chongqing, evening departure
- **Day 2**: Shore excursions, enter gorges
- **Day 3**: Three Gorges, more excursions
- **Day 4**: Three Gorges Dam, arrive Yichang

### Upstream (Yichang → Chongqing)
- Similar but reverse route
- Takes slightly longer due to current
- Often 4-5 days instead of 3-4

## Tipping Guidelines

- Not mandatory but appreciated
- Typical: ¥100-200 per person for crew (end of cruise)
- Individual tour guides: ¥20-50 per person per excursion
- Usually collected in group envelope

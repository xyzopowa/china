## Best Neighborhoods to Stay

### Tianhe District (天河区) ★★★★★

**Best For**: Shopping, modern amenities, convenience

**Character**: Modern business and shopping district, skyscrapers, upscale

**Pros**:
- Excellent metro connections (lines 1, 3, 5)
- Near Canton Tower
- Top shopping (Taikoo Hui, Grandview Mall)
- International restaurants
- Modern hotels
- Safe and clean

**Cons**:
- Less historical atmosphere
- Can feel impersonal
- More expensive dining

**Recommended For**: First-time visitors, business travelers, shoppers

**Key Areas**:
- **Zhujiang New Town**: Ultra-modern, luxury hotels
- **Tianhe Sports Center**: Central, good value
- **Teemall**: Shopping hub

### Shamian Island & Liwan District (沙面/荔湾) ★★★★★

**Best For**: Atmosphere, history, charm

**Character**: European colonial architecture, tree-lined streets, historic

**Pros**:
- Beautiful colonial buildings
- Quiet and peaceful island
- Near Shangxiajiu shopping street
- Traditional Cantonese culture
- Charming cafes and restaurants
- Photogenic

**Cons**:
- Fewer modern amenities
- Limited nightlife
- Fewer international chain hotels

**Recommended For**: Culture seekers, photographers, couples, repeat visitors

**Key Areas**:
- **Shamian Island**: Best atmospheric stay
- **Shangxiajiu**: Shopping and street food nearby

### Yuexiu District (越秀区) ★★★★

**Best For**: Central location, history, value

**Character**: Historical center, mix of old and new, local flavor

**Pros**:
- Near Beijing Road shopping
- Yuexiu Park
- Good value accommodation
- Local food scene
- Metro connections (lines 1, 2, 5, 6)
- Central to many attractions

**Cons**:
- Older buildings
- Can be crowded
- Less charm than Shamian

**Recommended For**: Budget travelers, central location seekers

**Key Areas**:
- **Beijing Road**: Shopping, convenient
- **Near Guangzhou Railway Station**: Budget options (but seedier area)

### Haizhu District (海珠区) ★★★

**Best For**: Canton Tower access, riverside

**Character**: Mix of old residential and new development

**Pros**:
- Near Canton Tower
- Pearl River views
- Quieter than city center
- Good local food
- Developing rapidly

**Cons**:
- Fewer attractions nearby
- Some areas still developing
- Can feel disconnected from main areas

**Recommended For**: Those focused on Canton Tower and riverside

### Baiyun District ★★

**Best For**: Near airport, not recommended for tourists

**Pros**:
- Near airport
- Budget options

**Cons**:
- Far from attractions
- Inconvenient
- Industrial areas

**Recommended For**: Early flights only

## Booking Considerations

### When to Book

**Canton Fair Periods** (April, October):
- Book 1-2 months ahead minimum
- Prices triple
- May sell out completely

**Chinese Holidays**:
- Book 2-4 weeks ahead
- Prices increase
- Chinese New Year, National Day (Oct 1-7)

**Normal Times**:
- 1-2 weeks ahead
- More flexibility
- Better prices

### What to Look For

1. **Metro access**: Essential for getting around
2. **Neighborhood character**: Modern vs. historic
3. **English service**: If needed
4. **Breakfast**: Western options available?
5. **Recent reviews**: Quality can decline
6. **Reliable Wi-Fi**: Check reviews

### Red Flags

- Very far from metro stations
- Lots of complaints about cleanliness
- Near Guangzhou Railway Station (seedier area, though improving)
- Extremely cheap (quality issues likely)
- No recent reviews

## What's Included/Expected

### International Hotels (4-5 Star)
- Daily housekeeping
- Toiletries and towels
- Fast Wi-Fi
- Breakfast (often)
- English-speaking staff
- Gym, pool
- 24-hour front desk
- Concierge service

### Mid-Range Chinese Hotels (3-4 Star)
- Basic housekeeping
- Basic toiletries
- Wi-Fi (may be slower)
- Chinese breakfast (usually included)
- Limited English
- Basic facilities

### Budget Hotels/Hostels
- Basic cleaning
- Bring own toiletries (many)
- Wi-Fi (quality varies)
- Breakfast rare or basic
- Minimal English
- No amenities

## Amenities Guide

### Breakfast

**International Hotels**:
- Buffet with Western and Asian options
- Coffee, juice, fruits
- Usually high quality
- Sometimes worth the hotel price alone

**Chinese Hotels**:
- Chinese breakfast: congee, buns, pickles
- Limited Western options
- Simpler spread
- Often included

**Budget**:
- Often not included
- Basic if offered
- Better to eat out

### Wi-Fi

- Standard in all hotels
- Quality varies greatly
- International hotels most reliable
- VPN may be needed for Western sites
- Ask for password at check-in

### Air Conditioning

- Essential in summer (hot and humid)
- Usually good in all hotels
- Can be very cold (bring layers)
- Heating in winter may be weaker

## Check-In Requirements

- **Passport**: Mandatory for foreigners
- **Registration**: Hotel registers you with police (automatic)
- **Deposit**: Sometimes required (cash or hold on card)
- **Check-in time**: Usually 2:00 PM
- **Check-out time**: Usually 12:00 noon

## Cost-Saving Tips

1. **Avoid Canton Fair periods**: Save 50-70%
2. **Book directly**: Sometimes cheaper than third-party
3. **Stay in Yuexiu**: Better value than Tianhe
4. **Weekdays cheaper**: Than weekends
5. **Longer stays**: Negotiate rate for 3+ nights
6. **Compare platforms**: Ctrip, Booking, Agoda, hotel direct

## Special Considerations

### Canton Fair Visitors
- Book months ahead
- Stay farther out (cheaper)
- Use metro to fair venue (Pazhou)
- Expect crowds

### Hong Kong Day-Trippers
- Consider not staying overnight
- 48-minute train from HK
- Or stay one night for food and sights

### Families
- Look for connecting rooms or suites
- International hotels more family-friendly
- Near parks (Yuexiu Park)

### Business Travelers
- Tianhe or Zhujiang New Town
- International chains
- Executive lounges
- Business centers

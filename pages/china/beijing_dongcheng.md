## Dongcheng District Overview

Dongcheng District is the historic center of Beijing, home to some of China's most iconic landmarks. This area represents imperial China at its finest, with centuries-old architecture and cultural treasures.

## Must-See Attractions

### Forbidden City (故宫)
**Duration**: 3-4 hours  
**Admission**: ¥60 (April-October), ¥40 (November-March)  
**Best Time**: Early morning to avoid crowds

The world's largest palace complex, home to 24 emperors during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Book tickets online in advance through the official website.

**Highlights**:
- Hall of Supreme Harmony
- Imperial Garden
- Clock and Treasure exhibitions
- Nine Dragon Wall

### Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)
**Duration**: 1 hour  
**Admission**: Free (ID required)

The world's largest public square, witness to many significant historical events. Located just south of the Forbidden City.

**Points of Interest**:
- Tiananmen Gate Tower
- Monument to the People's Heroes
- Mausoleum of Mao Zedong
- National Museum of China

### Temple of Heaven (天坛)
**Duration**: 2-3 hours  
**Admission**: ¥15 (park), ¥30 (complete ticket)

A masterpiece of Ming dynasty architecture where emperors performed annual ceremonies for good harvests.

**Highlights**:
- Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests
- Imperial Vault of Heaven
- Circular Mound Altar
- Echo Wall

## Additional Attractions

- **Jingshan Park**: Best views of the Forbidden City
- **Beihai Park**: Imperial garden with a white stupa
- **Lama Temple**: Active Tibetan Buddhist temple
- **Hutongs**: Traditional alleyway neighborhoods

## Dining Recommendations

- **Quanjude**: Famous Peking Duck restaurant
- **Dadong**: Modern take on Peking Duck
- **Ghost Street (Gui Jie)**: Night food street with endless options

## Getting There

- **Subway**: Line 1 (Tiananmen East/West stations)
- **Buses**: Multiple routes serve the area
- **Walking**: Most attractions within walking distance

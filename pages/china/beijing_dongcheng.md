## Dongcheng District Overview

Dongcheng District is the historic center of Beijing, home to some of China's most iconic landmarks. This area represents imperial China at its finest, with centuries-old architecture and cultural treasures.

### Temple of Heaven (Â§©Âùõ)

![Temple of Heaven](/images/beijing/temple_of_heaven.jpg)
*The iconic Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests*

Not only is the Temple of Heaven an architectural marvel, it is also a living slice of Beijing life. Built in the early 15th century during the reign of Emperor Yongle (Ming Dynasty), emperors performed annual sacrifices here to pray for good harvests. The complex's layout and details symbolise the cosmological link between heaven and earth.

**What to expect**:
- Gardens and open park space where locals practice tai chi, dance, and morning exercise ‚Äî great for candid cultural photos.
- The Echo Wall and Imperial Vault of Heaven are highlights; the Echo Wall transmits whispers clearly across its span.

**Booking**: Advance booking is recommended during busy periods; you can buy single-area tickets or combined tickets covering multiple halls. Common ticketing partners include Klook and local ticket portals.

More info: [Temple of Heaven on Visit Beijing](https://r.visitbeijing.com.cn/attraction/2) ‚Ä¢ [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temple_of_Heaven)

![Bell and Drum Tower](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1523473827532-3a7f2b1e9d4a?w=1200&q=80)
